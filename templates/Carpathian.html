Carpathian mountains, Ukraine

3 days, 2 nights in a tent, 47 km and 2700 m of elevation gain

Day 1. 15 km and 1300 m ⬆️

We started our 3 days adventure from the village of Kvasy (560 m) and the first summit we reached after 9 km was Stara mountain at 1472 m. a. s. l.

Stara mountain (Gora Stara) is a part of Carpathian Biosphere Reserve, World Network of Biosphere Reserves of UNESCO.

The hiking trails here are well equipped and pass through bilberry (european blueberry) and lingonberry bushes.

Day 2. 18 km and 500 m ⬆️

Second day of our adventure we started from Blyznytsia Mountain, 1881 m. a. s. l. It is the highest peak of the Svydivets massif.

The Svydovets (Ukrainian Свидівець; Russian Свидовец; Polish Świdowiec; Czech and Slovak Svidovec; German Swydiwez) is a mountain range in western Ukraine, one of the ranges of Eastern Beskids, itself belonging to the Outer Eastern Carpathians

Sunsets over mountains are different than those on the plains. The air immediately becomes wet and cold. Every part of you feels this bone-freezing agony.

Day 3. 18 km and 1000 m ⬇️

The whole day of hiking downhill to the village of Chorna Tysa- the hardest day of the adventure.

Lake Vorozheska is located on the northern slopes of the Svydovets massif at an elevation of 1460 meters above sea level. It has a glacial origin. The lake constitutes two connected bodies of water, a bigger and a smaller one. The water of the lake is clear and cool.
